critiqueit

A Web Application

The entrepreneur's online feedback community for preparing the winning pitch.

Gio heads to CritiqueIt

Our persona, Gio, heads to our site. Before she makes her account, Gio has the opportunity to understand what this application is and how to use it in a three slide carousel.

The Problem

Elevator pitches are difficult to give and perfect.

Just a few right and well-placed words can change a pitch from good to perfect, but without a defined feedback system on performance, presenters don’t have the tools to improve their elevator pitches.

Within the span of 30 seconds to a minute, or the amount of time it takes ride an elevator, your elevator pitch needs to describe and persuade someone to buy into your idea, product, event, or whatever it is you are trying to sell.

The success of an elevator pitch is gauged by whether or not the audience is convinced. A single defined "Yes" or "No" from an audience is helpful to some degree, but does not give proper feedback to pitchers of what they are doing wrong or right.

Our Persona

Meet Gio, our persona.

A young entrepreneur, Gio has one minute to pitch her web application idea to a panel of investors. It’s Tuesday night and she has until Friday to prepare. Others will also be presenting their ideas but only one will recieve funding.

Gio has three goals:

  1. Know her success rate
  2. Prepare quickly and efficiently
  3. Get detailed feedback

Gio struggles to clearly define the goals of her application. Though she has practices in front of the mirror, she has no idea if the panel will buy into her product.

Before building our persona, we interviewed a few people who we thought would find this application useful. Our interviewees, while having had to make pitches before, are often asked to present on short time notice.

Some of our interviewees included a health care startup founder, financial startup founder, and a business competition participant.

From those that we interviewed, we learned that overall they lacked a proper feedback network, had difficulty phrasing goals, and gauging audience interest. While all have had to make pitches in the past, oftentimes, they were asked to present with little time to prepare.

Process

In designing our product, we underwent rigorous iteration cycles.

First, we began with an ideation session where we hashed out ideas from all corners of extremity. We considered ideas that garnered towards diffierent goals such as ultra efficiency, comprehensive feedback, and binary feedback.

After considering all these ideas, we came up with our first design and made a paper prototype. We had two sets of paper prototypes, one set for our uploader's role (Gio), and one set for our reviewer's role (users who give Gio feedback). We put this paper prototype to test in six rounds of user testing, iterating as we went.

From there we went on to creating wireframes and a computer prototype. With each, we made sure to do a few rounds of user testing and design iteration to make sure we were on the right track.

Ultra Efficiency

Users give numerical feedback via the adjustable scale on the right side of the video screen. The higher the slider, the higher the current score.

Detailed Feedback

In this design, Gio decides what kind of feedback users can give. Some options may include grading scales, comment intervals, and acceptance of text-based comments.

Binary Feedback

Users cast a binary vote of whether or not they are sold on a pitch.

Uploader Scenario

  1. Upload your pitch
  2. View the feedback on your video
  3. View all your comments
  4. View comments at 1:00-1:10

Reviewer Scenario

  1. Watch a pitch
  2. Write a comment at 1:00
  3. Cast a vote
  4. Change your vote
