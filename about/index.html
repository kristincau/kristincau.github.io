On Design

I care a lot about the big picture. Before diving into the nitty gritty of design, it’s always worth thinking about whether or not the problem we’re trying to solve is the right problem(s) to solve. While good design takes into account usability and aesthetics, great design will also take into account business goals and technical constraints. At a high level, my design process goes something like this:

  1. Learn from everybody and everything

    2. User empathy paints part of the picture, but talking to stakeholders and engineers to learn what’s been done so far and why will eliminate surprises down the road.

  2. Process the learnings

    3. Distill learnings into something visual and tangible, like sketches, a mind map or wireframes, so everyone is on the same page.

  3. Take what you have and go back to Step 1

    4. Running with your head down might be faster, but it comes at the risk of going the wrong direction. The product of Step 2 should be taken back to Step 1 for re-evaluation via user testing or gathering more data.

Just as user empathy in a design is important, empathy for project collaborators is just as important. Every project is different and a design process should be flexible enough to accommodate a given situation.
